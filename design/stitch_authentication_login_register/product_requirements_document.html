Please provide me with the context! To create an effective Project Requirements Document (PRD) or a concise Project Brief, I need some information about the project itself. The quality of the PRD/brief will depend heavily on the details you provide. Please give me information regarding: 1. **Project Title/Name:** What do you call this project? 2. **The Core Idea/What it is:** Briefly describe what you're building or addressing. 3. **Problem it Solves / Opportunity it Addresses:** Why are we building this? What pain point does it alleviate, or what market opportunity does it tap into? 4. **Target Audience:** Who is this for? Describe the users/customers. 5. **Key Features/Functionality:** What are the main things this product/feature will do? (Even high-level bullet points are fine). 6. **Goals/Objectives:** What do we hope to achieve with this project? (e.g., increase user engagement, reduce churn, enter a new market, improve efficiency). 7. **Success Metrics (KPIs):** How will we measure if this project is successful? (e.g., X% increase in conversion, Y daily active users, Z average session duration). 8. **Assumptions, Constraints, or Dependencies:** Are there any known limitations (budget, timeline, technical), or things we're assuming to be true, or other projects it depends on? 9. **Current State/Background (if applicable):** Is this a new product, an enhancement to an existing one, or fixing something broken? 10. **Any other relevant information:** Competitive landscape, known risks, strategic alignment, etc. Once I have this context, I can structure it into a comprehensive PRD or a concise project brief, depending on your preference for detail.
